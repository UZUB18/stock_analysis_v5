# Basket Comparison Memo: NVDA vs PLTR
**Date:** 2026-03-11  

---

## Executive Summary
This relative capital allocation matrix evaluates the selected basket of AI-exposed assets: **NVIDIA (NVDA)** representing the infrastructure layer, and **Palantir (PLTR)** representing the application/software layer.

**The Verdict:** We are aggressively allocating towards the infrastructure layer (NVDA) due to its superior growth-to-valuation dynamic and undeniable cash generation, while maintaining a smaller tactical allocation in PLTR as a software-layer hedge despite its demanding multiple.

## The Stack Rank
1. **NVDA (85% Allocation):** The undisputed toll-collector of the AI era. Astonishingly, despite its market cap, it offers a far superior margin of safety on a forward earnings basis than its software peers.
2. **PLTR (15% Allocation):** An elite, mission-critical software business with structural lock-in, but currently priced for perfection with high execution dependency. 

## Comparative Valuation
There is a massive divergence in how the market is pricing these two assets relative to their growth:
- **NVDA:** Growing top-line at >70% with 65% operating margins, yet trading at a highly reasonable ~17x forward P/E. The market is pricing in an imminent cyclical crash in CapEx. If this crash is delayed, the stock is dramatically mispriced to the upside.
- **PLTR:** Growing top-line at ~20% with much lower (though improving) operating margins, yet trading at >65x forward P/E. It is priced as a hyper-growth asset without the hyper-growth fundamentals. 

*Quality-Adjusted Price:* NVDA offers a massive fundamental discount compared to PLTR. NVDA’s $96B in actual Free Cash Flow provides a tangible floor that PLTR simply does not possess yet.

## Relative Fragility
- **Who breaks first under stress?** **PLTR.**
- **Why?** PLTR's valuation requires flawless execution and zero macro deceleration. Any slowdown in US Commercial contract signings will cause a violent multiple compression. 
- **NVDA's Fragility:** NVDA is fragile to a sudden halt in hyperscaler CapEx (Meta, MSFT, Google). However, the "AI Arms Race" is currently a prisoner's dilemma for hyperscalers; stopping CapEx means losing the platform war, providing a short-term structural floor to NVDA's hardware demand.

## Execution Plan
- **Deploy 85% to NVDA immediately.** The 17x forward multiple is too cheap for a structural monopoly, even adjusting for cycle risks.
- **Deploy 15% to PLTR and hold.** We want exposure to the premier AI software pure-play, but we will not allocate heavier until the multiple compresses or revenue growth accelerates past 40%. 

## Anti-portfolio Note
*Why PLTR doesn't get 50%: * We refuse to pay a 65x forward P/E for 20% top-line growth. For PLTR to win our capital from NVDA, it must either (a) suffer a 40% price drawdown while maintaining fundamentals, or (b) re-accelerate revenue growth >45% consistently. 

## Review Triggers
- **For NVDA:** Hyperscaler earnings calls. If MSFT or Meta explicitly guide down AI infrastructure CapEx for two consecutive quarters, NVDA must be re-ranked.
- **For PLTR:** Monitor US Commercial revenue growth. If it drops below 30% YoY, the thesis breaks entirely.
